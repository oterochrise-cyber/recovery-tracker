The one rule

I measure success by what I do — not by what she does. Her warmth, silence, or choices are never scored and never predicted. A slip is data and a reset, not a failure.

Standards on contact
  • No relationship talks during the space period.
  • No "just checking in" / reassurance texts.
  • No asking coworkers or the assistant for intel.
  • No monitoring socials, location, gym, lunch, routes.
  • No physical contact unless she clearly initiates and welcomes it.
  • At work: polite, calm, brief, professional.
Anger control
  • The standard is: I do not act while angry. Feeling it is allowed.
  • No messages sent during a spike. Minimum 30-minute pause.
  • Draft important messages in Notes first.
  • When activated: Dad, walk, shower, eat, cold water, leave the room.
Reconnection standard (wks 5–6)
  • Don't initiate before six weeks unless she explicitly starts a relationship-level conversation.
  • Sustained regulation, not a few calm days. Independent movement from her.
  • Any reconnection is a new, slower relationship — not a snap-back.
"Hey [name], I've respected the space we agreed on and used the time to work seriously on myself. I'm not trying to pressure you or jump back into anything, but I'd be open to getting coffee and catching up if that's something you'd feel comfortable with."

After sending: accept the answer. No persuasion, no follow-up.
